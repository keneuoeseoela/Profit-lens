📚 What Amazon Owes You
📦 Lost Inventory Most Common
Units received at FC but disappear from inventory. Check Inventory Adjustments report (reason codes M, P, E).
💥 Damaged in Warehouse High Value
Amazon damages your product. Check the Inventory Adjustments report for reason code D (damaged).
💳 Fee Overcharges Easy Win
Wrong size tier applied = wrong fee charged. Compare your product dimensions to the fee charged in the FBA Fee report.
🔄 Returns Not Restocked Overlooked
Customer gets refund but item never returns to your inventory. Compare returns report to inventory additions.
🗑️ Destroyed Without Notice Check Monthly
Amazon auto-disposes inventory they flagged as unsellable. You must opt out or be reimbursed per unit.
✅ Monthly Audit Checklist
Download Inventory Adjustments Report
Seller Central → Reports → Fulfillment → Inventory Adjustments. Look for reason codes M, P, E, D.
Cross-check Returns vs Restock
Every refund issued should have a corresponding return. Any gap = potential reimbursement claim.
Audit FBA Fee Charges
Download FBA Fee Preview report. Compare dimensions used by Amazon vs your actual product dimensions.
Check Inbound Shipment Discrepancies
Compare units shipped (from your records) to units received in Seller Central. Any shortfall can be claimed.
Review Reimbursements Report
Seller Central → Reports → Payments → Reimbursements. Confirm all previously approved cases were paid.
File Cases Within 18-Month Window
Amazon has an 18-month statute of limitations on reimbursement claims. Don't let old cases expire unclaimed.
